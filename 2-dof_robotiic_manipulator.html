1. Mechanism Overview

This project already reads well from the resume. You designed a 2-DOF planar robotic arm in Simscape Multibody and used that as the basis for kinematic analysis and motion planning.

  • 2 joints make it simple enough to explain, but still rich enough for real robotics concepts.
  • The CAD model can be used to visually connect geometry with math.
  • It is ideal for demonstrating robotics fundamentals in a clear way.

2. Kinematics Stack

The resume mentions forward kinematics through a rigid body tree, inverse kinematics using the Levenberg-Marquardt numerical solver, and Jacobian-based velocity kinematics.

  • Forward kinematics maps joint angles to end-effector pose.
  • Inverse kinematics solves joint angles for a desired target position.
  • The Jacobian links joint rates to end-effector velocity.
  • This is the right place to show equations, blocks, and plots later.

3. Path Generation

You also generated smooth Cartesian paths using trapezoidal velocity profiles. That lets the page talk about motion quality, not just pose solving.

  • Targets can be geometric waypoints such as a square.
  • Trapezoidal profiles reduce sudden acceleration changes.
  • Motion planning becomes more realistic and less jerky.

  • Strong fit for robotics internships.
