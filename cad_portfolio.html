1. What This Page Represents

Since your resume already contains strong mechanical and design content, this page works as a general CAD portfolio hub. It is intentionally broad so that you can later drop in individual models, renders, and annotated drawings.

  • Best used for showcasing design process and not just final images.
  • Works well for SolidWorks renders, Simscape models, and analysis screenshots.
  • Can serve as the parent page for multiple subprojects.

2. Skills and Design Foundation

The resume lists SolidWorks, MATLAB, MATLAB Simulink, and ANSYS Forte in your technical skills. It also includes machine design, tolerances and fits, fatigue, fracture mechanics, CAD, and introductory FEA in your coursework.

  • Strong base for design-oriented internships and manufacturing support roles.
  • Shows knowledge of both geometry creation and engineering validation.
  • Lets you explain why your CAD choices are not just visual, but functional.

3. Suggested Portfolio Sections

A clean CAD portfolio page usually works best when it separates the work into categories. That makes the page feel organized and avoids looking like a random image dump.

  • Concept sketches and requirements.
  • Part modeling and assembly screenshots.
  • Motion study or kinematic checks.
  • Simulation results and design iterations.
  • Final takeaways and lessons learned.

4. Good Candidates from Your Resume

The strongest items to feature here are the 2-DOF robotic manipulator CAD model, the piston head geometries used in CFD, and any future drone or mechanism models you choose to publish.

  • 2-DOF robotic manipulator CAD and Simscape work.
  • CFD piston geometry comparison model.
  • Drone / fixture / mechanism sketches from robotics projects.
